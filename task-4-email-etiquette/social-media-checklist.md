# Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

## Responsible Social Media Use for College Students

| Do’s ✅ | Don’ts ❌ |
|--------|-----------|
| Think before posting any content online. | Do not share personal information such as phone number, address, or passwords. |
| Use respectful and professional language in posts and comments. | Do not post offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content. |
| Verify information before sharing it with others. | Do not spread fake news or unverified information. |
| Maintain privacy settings on your social media accounts. | Do not accept friend requests or messages from unknown people without caution. |
| Share content that reflects a positive and responsible online presence. | Do not post anything that may harm your reputation or future career opportunities. |

## Additional Good Practices
- Be polite and respectful in online discussions.
- Report cyberbullying, harassment, or suspicious activity.
- Avoid wasting too much time on social media during study hours.
- Use social media for learning, networking, and personal growth.

## Conclusion
Social media can be a powerful tool when used responsibly. By following these do’s and don’ts, college students can maintain a safe, respectful, and professional online presence.
